H5725 עֲדֻלָּם / עדלם

ʻĂdullâm

(ad-ool-lawm' | uh-dool-LAWM | uh-doo-LAHM)

Definition

proper locative noun; justice of the people; probably from the passive participle of the same as [H5724]; Adullam, a place in Palestine; Adullam.

  • a town of the Canaanites allotted to Judah and lying in the lowlands; site of the cave where David hid
