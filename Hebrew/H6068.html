H6068 עֲנָתוֹת / ענתות

ʻĂnâthôwth

(an-aw-thoth' | uh-naw-TOTE | uh-na-TOTE)

Definition

proper locative noun, proper masculine noun; answers to prayer; plural of [H6067]; Anathoth, the name of two Israelites, also of a place in Pal; Anathoth.

  • son of Becher and grandson of Benjamin (noun proper masculine)
  • one of the heads of the people who signed the covenant with Nehemiah (noun proper masculine)
  • a city of Benjamin allotted to the priest; located approximately 3 miles (5 kilometers) from Jerusalem; birthplace of the prophet Jeremiah (noun proper locative)
