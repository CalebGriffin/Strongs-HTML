H8656 תִּרְצָה / תרצה

Tirtsâh

(teer-tsaw' | tir-TSAW | teer-TSA)

Definition

proper feminine noun; favorable; from [H7521]; delightsomeness; Tirtsah, a place in Palestine; also an Israelitess; Tirzah.

  • one of the 7 daughters of Zelophehad the son of Hepher of the tribe of Manasseh (noun proper feminine)
  • one of the kingdoms on the west of the Jordan conquered by Joshua and the Israelites (noun proper locative)
  • a Canaanite city, later capital of the northern kingdom of Israel (noun proper locative)
