H8019 שְׁלֹמִית / שלמית

Shᵉlômîyth

(shel-o-meeth' | sheh-loh-MEET | sheh-loh-MEET)

Definition

proper feminine noun, proper masculine noun; peaceful; or שׁלוֹמִית; (Ezra 8:10), from [H7965]; peaceableness; Shelomith, the name of three Israelitesses; Shelomith.

  • a Gershonite Levite, son of Zichri in the time of David (noun proper masculine)
  • son of king Rehoboam of Judah by Maacah the granddaughter of Absalom (noun proper masculine)
  • head of a family of exiles who returned with Ezra (noun proper masculine)
  • a woman of Dan, daughter of Dibri, and mother of a son who blasphemed God and was stoned to death in the time of Moses (noun proper feminine)
  • daughter of Zerubbabel (noun proper feminine)
