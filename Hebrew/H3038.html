H3038 יְדוּתוּן / ידותון

Yᵉdûwthûwn

(yed-oo-thoon' | yeh-doo-TOON | yeh-doo-TOON)

Definition

proper masculine noun; praising; or יְדֻתוּן; or יְדִיתוּן; probably from [H3034]; laudatory; Jeduthun, an Israelite; Jeduthun.

  • a Levite of the family of Merari and the chief of one of the 3 choirs of the temple whose descendants also formed one of the perpetual temple choirs; he is named at the beginning of 3 Psalms ( Psa 39:1-13 , Psa 62:1-12 , 77) either as the composer or the recipient of those Psalms but most likely as the leader of the choir by which those Psalms were to be sung
