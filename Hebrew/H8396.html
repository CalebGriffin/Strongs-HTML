H8396 תָּבוֹר / תבור

Tâbôwr

(taw-bore' | taw-BORE | ta-VORE)

Definition

proper noun; mound; from a root corresponding to [H8406]; broken region; perhaps meaning "a stone-quarry" from the root תְּבַר; Tabor, a mountain in Palestine, also a city adjacent; Tabor.

  • a mountain in the plain of Esdraelon rising abruptly and insulated except for a narrow ridge on the west connecting it to the hills of Nazareth (noun proper mountain)
  • a town around the summit of Mount Tabor (1) (noun proper locative)
  • a city of the Merarite Levites located in the territory of Zebulun (noun proper locative)
  • the place of an oak tree which was on the homeward journey of Saul after he had been anointed by Samuel (noun proper arbour)
