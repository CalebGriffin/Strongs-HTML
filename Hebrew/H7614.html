H7614 שְׁבָא / שבא

Shᵉbâʼ

(sheb-aw' | sheh-BAW | sheh-VA)

Definition

proper locative noun, proper masculine noun; seven, an oath; of foreign origin; Sheba, the name of three early progenitors of tribes and of an Ethiopian district; Sheba, Sabeans.

  • son of Joktan and a descendant of Seth (noun proper masculine)
  • son of Raamah, grandson of Cush, and a descendant of Ham (noun proper masculine)
  • son of Jokshan, the son of Abraham by Keturah (noun proper masculine)
  • a nation in southern Arabia (noun proper locative)
